BHA Tech Strategy

Shaping the Future of IT at BHA

As Bush Heritage Australia scales its conservation impact across geographically dispersed reserves, our technology strategy must evolve. This briefing outlines the necessary transition from a traditional IT support model to an AI-enabled, data-driven architecture, and defines the crucial skills required for our new Head of IT to lead this transformation.

📊

The Data Foundation Challenge

Our "Standardised Methods" require semantic clarity. We currently lack a unified central data dictionary for entities like "Reserve," "Project," and "Site". To achieve national consistency and prevent a "data swamp," we must enforce strict schema and data quality at the point of entry, aligning closely with the MERG framework.

🤝

Leveraging the MSP

BHA relies on a Managed Service Provider (MSP) for day-to-day IT support and ticketing. The new Head of IT must strategically manage this vendor to handle baseline operations, freeing up internal capacity to architect the advanced data systems required for our geographically dispersed ecological operations.

Scaling Impact: The AI Implementation Path

AI is not a single tool, but a spectrum of capabilities requiring phased implementation, strong governance, and distinct skill sets. This section details our strategic roadmap, categorized by AI type and deployment phase, ensuring we prioritize based on ROI and technological readiness rather than chasing trends. Click on the phases below to explore specific BHA initiatives.

🔍

Predictive AI (Non-Generative)

The "Quick Win": Baseline Reserve Assessments

Utilizing Satellite imagery and Google Embeddings to rapidly assess new reserves.

Challenge: Defining methodology—whether models run nationally or are landscape-specific (utilizing specific spectral indexes for focal values responsive to local management effects).

🤖

Cognitive & Agentic AI (Generative)

Technical Productivity

Accelerating internal software development (S&D, Ag teams) using tools like Claude Code.

Requirement: Strict governance and code-management guidelines must be established first by the Head of IT.

Crucial Prerequisite: Regardless of the phase, Agentic workflows require documented processes and a single source of truth to prevent AI "hallucinations" and ensure ecological accuracy.

The Ideal Candidate: Architectural Enabler

Upper management must recognize that leading AI adoption does not necessarily mean writing AI algorithms. The ideal Head of IT is an architectural enabler. They build the data foundations (Data Lakes, schemas) and govern the processes that allow our ecologists and data scientists to deploy AI effectively. The chart below contrasts a traditional IT manager profile with the strategic profile required for BHA's future.

Traditional IT Manager

Focuses heavily on hardware maintenance, local server administration, and direct helpdesk ticketing. Often struggles to scale data infrastructure for advanced analytics.

Strategic Head of IT (BHA Ideal)

Leverages the MSP for standard support. Excels in cloud architecture (Azure), data governance, decoupling systems, and building the scalable data pipelines (Data Lakes) required to operationalize AI.
